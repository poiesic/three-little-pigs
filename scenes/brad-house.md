Brad heard the tripod before he saw it. A metallic scraping on the dirt path outside, followed by the unmistakable sound of Kevin clearing his throat the way he always did, like a garbage disposal working through a chicken bone.

"No way," Brad said. He set down his smoothie and pressed his snout to the window.

Kevin the Wolf stood in Brad's front yard, wrestling a ring light into position next to a camera on a tripod. He wore a tank top two sizes too small and had written "THE BIG BAD" across his chest in what appeared to be Sharpie. He squared up to the lens and shadowboxed the air twice.

"What's good, Dana?" Kevin said to the camera. "Kevin Wolf here. The Big Bad. You're about to witness something that's gonna change the game, bro. Lung capacity like you have never seen."

Brad backed away from the window. His beautiful, sustainable, carbon-negative straw house suddenly felt very thin. Because it was. It was straw.

"I should call Susan," he muttered.

Outside, Kevin was still going. "People talk about power. People talk about cardio. I got both, Dana. I got both. And I'm about to prove it live on camera with a feat of raw demolition that's gonna make your jaw hit the floor."

Kevin turned from the camera and locked eyes on Brad's house. He rolled his neck. Cracked his knuckles. Flexed both arms and kissed each bicep.

"Yo, little pig!" Kevin bellowed. "Open up. I'm trying to shoot something here."

Brad opened the front door a crack. "Bro, I'm not really in a place where I can have visitors right now. Energy's off. Mercury's in retrograde."

"I don't need to come in. I need you to come out. Or don't. Doesn't matter." Kevin jerked a thumb at the camera. "This is my audition tape for Dana White. UFC. Professional. You know what that means?"

"That you're trespassing?"

Kevin ignored him. "It means I need content showing what I can do. And what I can do, little pig, is blow this house down."

Brad looked at his walls. Straw. Locally sourced, hand-bundled, beautiful golden straw. He'd given a TEDx talk about it at the Piglet Pines community center. Fourteen people came. Well, eleven, but three of them were really engaged.

"Bro, you can't just destroy someone's house for a video."

"Watch me." Kevin turned back to the camera, pointed at Brad's house with both index fingers. "Dana, what you're about to see is one hundred percent real. No CGI. No tricks. Pure wolf power."

He took a stance. Feet wide. Chest out. Drew in a breath so deep his tank top rode up past his belly button.

And blew.

The front wall went first. Just peeled away like wet paper. The straw scattered across the yard in a golden cloud, and the rest of the house followed. Walls folding, roof collapsing, the whole thing flattening in about four seconds. Brad's smoothie hit the dirt. His yoga mat rolled into the road.

Brad stood in the middle of what used to be his living room, straw in his hair, his furniture exposed to the open sky like a dollhouse with the front ripped off.

Kevin pumped both fists. "LET'S GO! You see that, Dana? One breath! ONE BREATH!" He ran to the camera and pointed into the lens. "That's the kind of athlete you're getting. That's Big Bad energy right there."

Brad didn't wait for the rest of the speech. He grabbed his phone and his sandals and started walking toward Terence's place. Behind him, Kevin was doing a victory lap around the debris pile, narrating his own highlight reel.

"Bro," Brad said to no one, "this day is so off-brand."
